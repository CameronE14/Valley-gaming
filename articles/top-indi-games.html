Indi Gems You Need To Dig Up

Take a look at some of my favourite Indi games.

5. Super Meat Boy

Tight controls and even tighter level design. There's even a boat load of content. By the last level you'll be pulling out all sorts of tips and tricks that you didn't even realise you knew.

4. Celeste

This excels in all the way Super Meat Boy does, but add a killer soundtrack and story and you've got a fantastic little game. Lots of levels and some crasy b-sides. It's an absolute joy to play.

3. Undertale

Toby Fox is a genius and the fact only one person managed to make a game like it, blows my mind. It's a quirky game that should have all the praise its getting. I'm looking forward to the next chapter in Toby Fox's career with Deltarune.

2. Outer Wilds

Time-loop baby! This is one of those games I didnt expect a lot from, but when I finally give it a try I was amazed by the charm and intricacies of the title. Every little puzzle adds up to a huge pay-off. This is one of those games I wish I could forget about, just so I could experience it again.

1. Hollow Knight

A mixture of my two favourite genres, Souls-like and Metroidvania. This charming title has it all. Great art, Music and Gameplay. With a sequel in Hollow Knight: Silksong coming any time now, I can't wait to experience more in Deepnest and beyond.
