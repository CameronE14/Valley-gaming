The Top 10 Games of All Time (In my opinion)

A run down of the greatest games of all time. Though, this is completely opinion based and you'll have a different list than mine. I'd like to celebrate my love for these games by giving them some attention.

10. Hollow Knight

As indi games go, this has everything. A mysterious atmosphere, deep lore, incredible music and tight gameplay. I'd consider this a mixture of 2 genres; Souls-like & Metroidvania, with anyone thats played a Souls game understanding the death mechanic and probably being comfortable with difficulty curve, and fans of Metroidvania will love the open exploritory nauture of the game. With interesting characters, art-style that pops every chance it can get, and the different biomes that feel very unique. You'll find yourself lost in one of the greatest indi games available right now.

9. Zelda: Ocarina of Time

THis game blew me away as a kid. I couldn't get over the sheer scale of it. It was actually my first introduction to Zelda, and what an introduction it was. This'll probably make a lot of peoples lists, but for me its the music. Ocarina of Time has one of the most addictive soundtracks (Gerudo Valley) and some really catchy tunes that I still hum to this day.

8. Chrono Trigger

The fact there hasn't been a game like Chrono Trigger since it came out is testiment to the masterclass that it is. Its a shame there hasnt been a re-release of this game on a modern console, because it needs to be experienced by modern audiences. A JRPG that has you journeying through time from the prehistoric to the ruined future, wrapped in art from Akira Toriyama (DBZ) and a great soundtrack, In my opinion this is the best 16-bit game of its time.

7. Half-Life 2

When I say Half-Life 2, what I actually mean is everything that came with it. Yes, its a great game on its own. But we need to remember when buying HL2 you got Counter Strike: Source and Half-Life 2: Deathmatch. Two absolutley increadible games alongside what people consider one of the best FPS games ever released at that point.

6. World of Warcraft

WoW has taken a knock as of late, but that doesn't diminish what it's achieved. Before WoW, MMORPGs weren't exactly widely known or played. The biggest was probably EverQuest. The technological leap between these two games was massive. Nobody had ever seen a game like WoW before and it blew the competition out of the water. With an already established brand and story people were already invested before even making thier first character. With an emphasis on group activity, people actually had to talk to other players to get stuff done. It had a massive world and lots of content that people would sink thousands of hours into.

5. Dark Souls

I could easily list Bloodborne or any of the other souls games here. But for me, this is the game that hooked me. There's nothing like beating that one boss you've been stuck on for days or even weeks. There's a real sense of skill progression and you can feel yourself getting better at the game with every "VICTORY ACHIEVED" Let's not forget this game is set in a fantastical medieval world with knights, dragons and magic which adds to the brutality of the game.

4. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt

There's no game quite like The Witcher 3. The setting and story keep players engrossed with tight and sometimes difficuly combat to really give the game some flair. Graphically it was easily the best thing around at release and with the difinitive edition coming to the current gen, I look forward to seeing this game back in the limelight.

3. Final Fantasy XIV: A Realm Reborn

Words cannot describe how incredible this game is. The story, the music, the community. The developers have turned this into something special. Every expansion builds on the game and really gives the players a special experience. It has well balanced gameplay and tightly designed multiplayer content. The world is massive too, with it being mostly instanced it gives each zone a real unique feeling with something to see in each corner of Eorzea.

2. Disco Elysium

There's currently nothing like Disco Elysium. I tried this on the off chance that I may like it and it blew me away. Revachol is the setting for a brutal murder and its up to our alchohol induced amnesiac detective to crack the case. There's lots of ways to play Disco Elysium and all of them are correct, with each playthrough being unique if you want it to be. This is THE game I recommend to anyone looking for something different.

1. Final Fantasy VII

No surprise here I guess, someone who's a massive fan of JRPGs is putting FF7 at the top of this list. But come on, this game is great. I can't expres how much this game means to me and other fans. This was my introduction to JRPGs and Final Fantasy as a whole. I haven't got anything insightful to say about FF7 that hasn't been said by someone else, other than I believe this game is very important to not just players, but the entire industry and really changed the way stories were told.
